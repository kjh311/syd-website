Larisa Babak

 

Executive Assistant


lbabak@templebnaitorah.org

Born in Ukraine, Larisa grew up in America with a sense of straddling two worlds—balancing her legacy of the former Soviet Union with that of growing up in the West. From this world-view sprang a deep curiosity and love of learning about people from all walks of life and backgrounds. In her perusals of the different historical backgrounds and religions of people all over the world, Larisa was especially drawn to the history of Israel and the role they have played throughout the ages. Naturally, she gravitated toward a position on the team of staff at Temple B’nai Torah. You can usually find Larisa doing any number of things that she loves most—entirely engrossed in a fascinating new book, lifting heavy weights at the gym, hiking trails around the beautiful PNW, or if you can’t find her at all, it’s because she’s off traveling to another country on her bucket list.

Josh Niehaus

 

Director of Music, Marketing & Membership


jniehaus@templebnaitorah.org

Josh Niehaus is a musician, song-leader, lifelong Jewish "camp guy," and the current artist-in-residence at Temple B'nai Torah. Josh studied music at Columbia University (although he majored in history) and received an additional degree in audio engineering from the Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences. After graduating, Josh worked as the music assistant on the Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien. Throughout that time, Josh's love for Jewish music and liturgy began to develop. He started attending Jewish music conferences and set up apprenticeships to study with various cantors and rabbis to develop his skills as a shaliach tzibbur (Jewish prayer leader). Josh transitioned to a full-time synagogue position in 2012 as the musical director at Temple Sholom in Vancouver, BC. 

Over the past decade, Josh has worked at numerous synagogues in both the Reform and Conservative movements, and he was the former camp director of the Stroum Jewish Community Center. Josh is one half of the musical duo Josh and Chava, and he continues to work in the world of Jewish camping as the Associate Director of Camp Solomon Schechter. Josh lives with his wife, Karli, and their puppy Mylo, in North Seattle. 

Nison Simontov

 

Director of Administration


nsimontov@templebnaitorah.org

We are very pleased to welcome Nison Simontov as our new Director of Administration! Although Nison has called Seattle home for the past thirty years, his story begins in Tashkent, Uzbekistan. After a secular upbringing during the Soviet regime, Nison and his family emigrated to the United States in 1992. Upon arriving in America, Nison was granted an opportunity to begin his Jewish education at the Seattle Hebrew Academy and later, the Northwest Yeshiva High School. Nison later attended the University of Washington and graduated with a degree in Political Science and obtained a law degree.

In his professional life, Nison founded a home health care agency to help families struggling to find suitable care for their loved ones. During this time, he was also instrumental in developing a non-profit foundation aimed at funding medical research for Alzheimer’s disease at the UCLA School of Medicine. Nison worked at two separate start-ups, a gourmet tea company and a telemedicine company. At the onset of the Covid pandemic, he was asked to manage a retirement home in Everett, WA.to ensure continued financial growth and safety of residents and staff.

Nison is a father to a 15-year-old son, Noah, and an adorable 4 year-old sassy Yorkie, named Gigi. He enjoys writing and directing plays and film shorts, painting, and is trying his best not to injure himself while practicing his most recent hobby, woodworking. He is excited about joining our community and contributing to its success.
